Strategic Objectives

  • 1. Develop a data-driven strategy Design an integrated system that uses open data (conflict events, population distribution, health, school locations, displacement, and environmental indicators) to identify how conflict dynamics affect education access and to prioritize schools and communities most at risk in conflict-affected areas.
  • 2. Create geospatial monitoring tools Create interactive maps, dashboards and mapping tools that visualize education risk (e.g., conflict proximity, historic conflict event, accessibility disruption, population pressure) and support real-time monitoring of schools before, during, and after conflict events, enabling field teams and community to validate and act on insights.
  • 3. Build EBI staff capacity for scalable use Train staff and community members to interpret spatial risk outputs, use monitoring dashboards effectively, and replicate the methodology across different regions and infrastructure types, ensuring the system can be independently scaled and adapted in humanitarian sectors like health, food security and others without redesigning from scratch.

Methodology & Transparency

Our system utilizes a unified data pipeline aggregating conflict data from ACLED and UCDP GED. Schools are geolocated using GRID3 and OSM datasets. Risk classification is determined via an automated Haversine distance analysis, flagging any facility within a 10km radius of an active conflict event recorded in our 20-year historical database.
